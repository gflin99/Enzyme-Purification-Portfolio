# One-Step Purification of Thermostable Carbonic Anhydrases (BEST 2022 Showcase)

This repository hosts the downstream bioprocessing research on the cost-effective, one-step purification of thermostable carbonic anhydrases ($Ssp\text{CA}$ and $Saz\text{CA}$) from *Sulfurihydrogenibium yellowstonense* and *Sulfurihydrogenibium azorense*. This work was presented at the **27th Annual Meeting of the Biological Engineering Society of Taiwan (BEST 2022)**.

本儲存庫記錄了我於碩士入學前暑假提前參與的蛋白質與酵素純化專案。此研究成果隨後發表於中華民國生物技術與生物工程學會年會（BEST 27th），為我日後在生物工程領域奠定了紮實的實驗與研究基石。

## 📄 Academic Abstract 

**Title:** One-step purification of thermostable carbonic anhydrases from *Sulfurihydrogenibium yellowstonense* and *Sulfurihydrogenibium azorense*  
**Authors:** Chung-Fu Lin (Isaac Lin), Chia-Jung Hsieh, Chi-Yang Yu*  
**Institution:** Department of Chemical Engineering and Biotechnology, Tatung University, Taipei City, Taiwan  

> **Background:** Carbonic anhydrases (CA) exist widely in various organisms and catalyze the reversible hydration of carbon dioxide into bicarbonate and protons. CA can be applied to industrial carbon sequestration, where thermostable enzymes such as CA from *Sulfurihydrogenibium yellowstonense* ($Ssp\text{CA}$) and *Sulfurihydrogenibium azorense* ($Saz\text{CA}$) are especially suitable. Conventionally, recombinant $Ssp\text{CA}$ and $Saz\text{CA}$ are purified using Immobilized Metal Affinity Chromatography (IMAC). However, large-scale purification via IMAC is unpractical for industrial applications due to high costs. In this work, the feasibility of purifying $Ssp\text{CA}$ and $Saz\text{CA}$ through a simplified, one-step heating process was examined.
>
> **Results:** Recombinant $Ssp\text{CA}$ and $Saz\text{CA}$ were expressed in induced *E. coli* BL21(DE3) cells carrying $\text{pET28a(+)}-Ssp\text{CA}$ and $\text{pET28a(+)}-Saz\text{CA}$ plasmids, respectively. To optimize the purification, the cell suspensions were heated at 70°C, 80°C, and 90°C for durations of 5, 10, 15, and 20 minutes. For $Saz\text{CA}$, the highest enzyme activity (944 WAU/mL) and highest purity (59.7%) were achieved under a 5-minute heating treatment at 80°C. For $Ssp\text{CA}$, the highest enzyme activity (1046 WAU/mL) was observed after 5 minutes of heating at 90°C, while the maximum purity (63.0%) was obtained after 20 minutes of heating at 80°C.
>
> **Conclusions:** This report demonstrates that cell disruption and the precipitation of host constitutive proteins can both be successfully achieved through a direct heating process, entirely bypassing the conventional, energy-consuming prior sonication step. The feasibility of a highly simplified, low-cost downstream purification process was successfully demonstrated, offering a viable strategy for industrial-scale enzyme production.

## 🔬 Core Techniques & Methodology 
不同於傳統複雜的蛋白質純化，本專案的核心在於**「製程簡化」與「熱動力學分離」**：
*   **Thermal Cell Lysis (熱誘導細胞破碎)：** 利用高溫（70°C–90°C）直接破壞大腸桿菌細胞壁，釋放目標重組蛋白，免除超音波破碎步驟。
*   **Differential Precipitation (差異性熱沉澱)：** 利用 $Ssp\text{CA}$ 與 $Saz\text{CA}$ 極佳的耐熱特性，在高溫下使大腸桿菌本身的雜蛋白變性並沉澱，透過一階離心即可獲得部分純化的酵素。
*   **Enzyme Assay (酵素活性測定)：** 進行威爾伯-安德森（Wilbur-Anderson）活性分析，定量高溫處理後的酶催化效率。
